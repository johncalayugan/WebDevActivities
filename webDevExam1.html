The brief hisory of Japan

Overview of timeline

Japan was settled about 35,000 years ago by Paleolithic people from the Asian mainland. At the end of the last Ice Age, about 10,000 years ago, a culture called the Jomon developed. Jomon hunter-gatherers fashioned fur clothing, wooden houses, and elaborate clay vessels.

Timeline of Japan 

        
            i. Ancient Japan 
               
                    The prehistoric and ancient era of Japan covers the time up until 538 AD, including the Paleolithic 
            (from 35,000 BC to 14,000 BC), Jomon (from 14,000 BC to 300 BC), Yayoi (from 300 BC to 250 AD), and Kofun 
            (from 250 to 538) periods.

            

            ii. Classical Japan
                Classical Period (538-1185)

            The Classical Period opened with a big leap in the political, social, and artistic aspects of the 
            Japanese society, including the fruition of the Yamato rule and the beginnings of Buddhism in Japan. 
            This formative stage is known as the Asuka period.
